Geography

Sousse occupies a geographic location in central Tunisia , on the Sahel coastline overlooking the Mediterranean Sea bordering the east of the country. The city covers 45 km 2 and is 25 meters above sea level.

History

If the Peoples of the sea were probably previously established in the region of Sousse, it is to the Phoenicians that the first known name of the city is attributed. In the xi th century BC. AD appears the toponym Hadrim which designates, according to M'hamed Hassine Fantar 10 , an enclosure or a residential area. Archaeological remains of the site does not go back beyond the vi th century BC. AD , period when Hadrim came under the authority of Carthage and lived with it the Punic warswhile maintaining a Phoenician identity as evidenced in particular by local funeral practices. After losing the battle of Zama , Hannibal Barca , who has properties in the vicinity of Hadrim, has his soldiers carry out civil works and is responsible for the planting of many olive trees in the region.

Demography

The population of the municipality of Sousse reached 221,530 inhabitants in 2014 2 for a density of nearly 5,000 inhabitants / km 2 while its agglomeration (Grand Sousse) was around 400,000 inhabitants in 2004 6 , 690,000 in 2015 then 710,000 inhabitants in 2017 , which places it in fourth position in the agglomerations of the country.
